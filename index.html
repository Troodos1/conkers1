How to play

The idea. Two conkers duel. You take turns: on your turn you strike the other conker; on their turn you brace against their blow. First to smash the other wins.

Striking

Touch mode: press and drag down & back from your conker. The further you pull, the more power — but a big pull sways your aim, so it's a trade‑off. The angle you drag sets the direction. Release to let fly.

Tilt + pull mode: tilt the phone left/right to aim, drag back with your finger for power only, release to strike.

Your conker takes a moment to fly, and the target is moving — so you must lead it: aim where the target will be, not where it is. Damage = power × accuracy × timing. The hardest hits land when the pale scar faces you as the conker arrives (the “sweet spot” — a faint ring pulses when it's lined up).

Bracing

When it's the other conker's turn, watch for the wind‑up warning, then tap right as the swing lands to brace. Timing is everything: a perfectly‑timed brace blocks almost everything and even rebounds damage back. Tap too early and you flinch — wasting it. Mistime it and you take the blow flush.

Tactics

• Big power isn't always best — a wild, hard swing is easy to miss. A controlled, well‑timed hit on the sweet spot often does more.
• Be patient on defence. Don't mash the screen — wait for the actual swing.
• Watch the target's rhythm and fire ahead of it.

Difficulty

Changes how fast the target sways: Sitter (dead still) → Easy → Medium → Hard (fast). A faster target is harder to lead and time. In 2‑player the Host sets it for both.

Ranks (they stick)

Win and your conker hardens up a rank (Fresh → Seasoned → … → King), gaining life; lose and it softens down one rank (never below the bottom). Your rank is saved on your phone and carries across every game and mode until you derank. A Best‑of series ranks you up or down once for the whole series.

Handicap (underdog aid)

In Controls you can switch on a handicap: when you're a lower rank than your opponent you take 5% less damage per rank you're below them (up to 50% less). It only ever helps the underdog, and a 🛡 badge shows by both names so the other player knows.

King's taunt

Reach the top rank (King) and a cheeky 😈 Taunt button appears during a bout — fling a bit of friendly trash‑talk at your opponent.

2‑player

In Controls → 2 Players, choose how to connect:

• Nearby (offline) — duel someone in the same room with no internet (great on a plane). One phone Hosts, the other taps Join and finds it automatically. Needs Bluetooth, Wi‑Fi and Location switched on.
• Online (internet) — duel a friend anywhere. The Host shows a 4‑letter code; the other types it in. Both need internet.
